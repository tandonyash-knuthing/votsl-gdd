The Vigil of the
Second Light

"A High-Precision 2D Action-RPG where a father must become the shield, so his daughter can become the storm."

Trust Mechanic

Asymmetric combat. The Hero protects the Child to maintain her focus. Failure breaks the bond.

Hope-Gating

Progression tied to healing. Defeating trauma unlocks physical abilities.

Soul-Forged Choice

Act III splits into two radically different gameplay experiences: Hard Mode vs. Power Fantasy.

Theme: Duty vs. Delusion

The game explores the concept of "Vigil" (duty to the dead) versus the "Illusion of Solace" (escaping grief through madness).

Act I: The Journey to Betrayal

  • Hero begins alone, suffering from "Depression" (Mechanically Nerfed).
  • Climax (H3): The Priest reveals the Elf is truly dead.
  • The Reveal: Hero finds the King's Letter, confirming the Child he protects is his Daughter.

Act II: The Stewardship

  • The Hero accepts his role as Father and Protector.
  • Gameplay Shift: The Child joins combat (Trust System unlocked).
  • They travel to elemental domains to unlock her magic.

Core Mechanics

The Trust System (Act II)

The Hero focuses on defense so the Daughter can focus on offense.

  • Hero: Tank / Parry / Positioning
  • Daughter: Ranged Elemental Artillery
  • Panic State: Missed Parry = 5s Silence (0 DPS)

Act III: The Crossroads of Fate

The King (Demon of Hatred) has kidnapped the Daughter. The Hero finds the Soul-Forged Blade.

Path A: The Good Path

Reject the Blade (Hard Mode)

The Hero accepts the Elf's death and refuses the temptation of power.

  • Gameplay: Solo. No Ranged Attacks. Rely on Movement & Melee.
  • Goal: Rescue the Daughter to regain magic.
  • Narrative: Sanity & Truth.

Path B: The Bad Path

Accept the Blade (Power Fantasy)

The Hero succumbs to the delusion that the Elf is alive in the sword.

  • Gameplay: Rapid Fire Ranged Corruption. No Cooldowns.
  • Goal: Slaughter the King.
  • Narrative: Madness & Callousness. Child Trust Disabled.

The Three Endings

Good Ending

Vigil of the Second Light

Path A + Defeat King. The Daughter is rescued. They live in peace. She calls him "Father."

Normal Ending

The Cycle Ends

Path A + Hero Dies. The Daughter wields his blade to defeat the King alone, carrying his spirit.

Bad Ending

Illusion of Solace

Path B. Hero slaughters King. Daughter abandons him. He sits on the throne, mad with grief.
